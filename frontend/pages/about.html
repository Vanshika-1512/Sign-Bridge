AI Pipeline

From pixels to words

SignBridge works entirely in your browser using WebAssembly-compiled computer vision models. No video is ever sent to our servers — your privacy is preserved by design.

Each animation frame captured from your webcam goes through a multi-stage pipeline: hand detection, landmark extraction, feature engineering, and finally gesture classification.

The classifier maps a 63-dimensional vector of hand landmark coordinates to one of the 26 ASL letters, using a lightweight neural network fine-tuned on thousands of gestures.

1

Frame Capture

Browser MediaStream API captures your webcam at 30fps. Each frame is drawn to a canvas and passed to MediaPipe.

2

Hand Detection

MediaPipe Hands locates the hand bounding box and extracts 21 3D landmark points (fingertips, knuckles, wrist).

3

Feature Extraction

Landmark coordinates are normalized relative to the wrist, making detection scale- and position-invariant.

4

Gesture Classification

A trained model (TensorFlow.js / rule-based classifier) maps features to one of 26 ASL letters with a confidence score.

5

Text + TTS Output

Predicted letters are accumulated into words. Web Speech API optionally reads them aloud.

The Model

A gesture classifier built for the browser

Rather than processing raw pixels, SignBridge uses a two-stage approach: first, MediaPipe extracts normalized hand landmark coordinates; then, a compact neural network classifies the pose.

This design runs entirely client-side with zero latency, as no video data leaves your device. The model was trained on the ASL Alphabet dataset (Kaggle) and fine-tuned with custom-collected samples for edge cases.

Accuracy varies by lighting conditions, hand size, and skin tone. We are continually collecting diverse samples to improve equity across demographic groups.
